FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about window tinting

How long does the window tinting process take?

Most vehicles can be completed in 2-3 hours depending on the type of vehicle and the package selected. We offer same-day service for most jobs, and you can wait in our comfortable waiting area or get a ride back to work/home and return when your vehicle is ready.

What is the darkest legal tint in South Carolina?

In South Carolina, the legal limit for front windows is 27% VLT (Visible Light Transmission). Rear side windows and rear windows can be any darkness. We always stay within legal limits unless specifically requested otherwise, and we'll provide guidance on what's legal for your vehicle.

What's the difference between ceramic, carbon, and standard tint?

Standard tint is dyed film that provides basic UV protection and privacy. Carbon tint uses carbon particles to block heat and provides a matte finish. Ceramic tint is our premium option, using ceramic particles to block up to 80% of heat while allowing maximum clarity and not interfering with electronic signals. Each has different price points and performance characteristics.

How long should I wait before rolling down my windows after tinting?

We recommend waiting at least 3-5 days before rolling down your windows to allow the tint to fully cure. The exact time can vary based on weather conditions and the type of film used. We'll provide you with specific care instructions when you pick up your vehicle.

Do you offer a warranty on your window tinting?

Yes, all of our window tint installations come with a lifetime warranty against peeling, bubbling, or discoloration. Our premium ceramic films also include manufacturer warranties. We stand behind our work and will address any issues promptly.
